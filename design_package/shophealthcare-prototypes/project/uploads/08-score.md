# Direction 08 — The Score

**Thesis:** Credit Karma proved people will check their credit score obsessively if you make it free, fast, and shareable. Spotify Wrapped proved people will share their data publicly if you make it beautiful and identity-affirming. Nobody has done this for health insurance. Your "Coverage Score" — a 0-100 number that tells you how good your plan actually is — becomes the hook, the product, and the viral loop. You don't sell insurance. You give people a number. The number sells the insurance.

**Analog brands:** Spotify Wrapped (annual data reveal as cultural event), Credit Karma (free score as top-of-funnel), BeReal (authentic sharing mechanic), Apple Health (clean data visualization), Duolingo (streak/score gamification), Robinhood (portfolio performance cards), NerdWallet (financial score + recommendations).

**Why this is disruptive:** No insurance company gives you a score. They give you a plan name and a premium. That's like a bank giving you an account number instead of a balance. The Coverage Score translates insurance complexity into a single, understandable, shareable number. "I got a 34. What did you get?" — that's a conversation nobody is having about insurance. We start it.

**TAM play:** PC-1 primary (23M+ passive/complacent — the score creates awareness of a problem they didn't know they had). Viral reach across ALL personas through social sharing. SP-1 secondary (data-oriented, will want to optimize their score).

---

## Positioning moment

> A massive "34" with a score gauge. "That's your Coverage Score. Most people score higher. Want to see why?"

The homepage is a score reveal. Not a headline. Not a photo. A number that makes you curious, slightly uncomfortable, and eager to understand.

---

## Decision 1 — Color

**Background — "Cloud"** `#FAFAF9` (warm near-white, app-like)
**Primary — "Indigo"** `#6366F1` (the Score color — unique in insurance, nobody owns purple/indigo)
**Score gradient:** `#EF4444` (0-30) → `#F59E0B` (31-60) → `#22C55E` (61-85) → `#6366F1` (86-100)
**Accent — "Amber"** `#F59E0B` (CTAs, highlights)
**Text — "Graphite"** `#1C1917` (warm dark body)
**Text Secondary** `#78716C` (muted labels)
**Card surfaces** `#FFFFFF` with subtle shadow (the only direction using pure white cards)

**Rule:** The score gradient is sacred. Red-amber-green-indigo maps to plan quality. Every data viz uses this gradient. It becomes instantly recognizable.

---

## Decision 2 — Typography

**Headline:** **Sora** — geometric, modern, slightly playful. Rounded terminals.
**Body:** **DM Sans** — clean, friendly.
**Data/labels:** **Geist Mono** — score numbers, plan details.
**The Score itself:** **Sora at 120px+** — the number IS the design.

**Scale:**
- Score display: 96 / 120 — Sora, 700
- Hero: 40 / 56 — Sora, 700
- H1: 28 / 40 — Sora, 600
- Body: 16 / 17 — DM Sans, 400, line-height 1.6
- Label: 12 / 14 — Geist Mono, 500

---

## Decision 3 — Nora

**Form:** Friendly illustrated avatar with Indigo accent ring. The "score interpreter" — like a fitness coach reading health data.
**Opening:** "Your Coverage Score is 34. That's below average for Texas. The biggest issue: your plan's network doesn't include your primary care doctor. Want me to find plans that do?"
**Personality:** Curious · Encouraging · Data-literate · Non-judgmental · Specific

---

## Decision 4 — Photography

Minimal. The data visualization IS the visual. Score gauges, gradient bars, shareable cards replace photography. One lifestyle photo allowed in social proof section — person looking at phone with a smile (the "I just checked my score" moment).

---

## Decision 5 — Voice

1. Lead with curiosity. "What's your Coverage Score?" not "Find better insurance."
2. The score is the hook. Everything else is secondary.
3. Shareable language: "My coverage score is 34. Yours?" — design for screenshot.
4. No shame. A low score isn't failure — it's information.
5. Zero commitment: Free. Fast. No signup to see your score.
6. Social proof is score-based: "The average American scores 47. SHC members average 82."
7. Nora interprets: "Your 34 breaks down to: Cost 45, Network 28, Coverage 31."
8. Annual recap (Wrapped mechanic): "Your 2026 Coverage Recap: Score went from 34 to 87."
9. Updates re-engage: "Your Coverage Score changed. New plans available."
10. Every number is shareable. Design for the screenshot.

---

## The Shareable Card

The most important design element. After scanning, users get a "Coverage Score Card" — like Spotify Wrapped — showing:
- Their score (big number)
- Score breakdown (3-4 bars)
- How they compare to average
- CTA: "Check yours at shophealthcare.io"

Designed for Instagram Stories, Twitter/X, and iMessage. THIS is the viral loop.

---

## Risk

- **Score validity.** If the Coverage Score feels arbitrary, the whole direction collapses. Needs a defensible methodology.
- **Comparison to credit scores.** Credit scores have decades of cultural entrenchment. Coverage Score is invented. Adoption isn't guaranteed.
- **Gaming.** If people figure out the score formula, they'll game it instead of finding the right plan.
- **Requires product engineering.** The score algorithm must exist before the brand can launch.

---

## Best suited for

PC-1 primary (curiosity-driven acquisition). SP-1 secondary (optimization mindset). Viral reach across all personas through social mechanics. BR-1 may find it too playful.
