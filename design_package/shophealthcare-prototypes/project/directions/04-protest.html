THE FIVE TACTICS
WE REFUSE TO USE.

These are industry-standard. We threw them out on day one. If we ever use them, call us out in public — we'll print the correction.

01.

Dark-pattern countdowns

"Only 2 plans left at this price." "Price expires in 2:47." Manufactured scarcity designed to spike your cortisol and force the click. It's the oldest trick in e-commerce. It has no place in health insurance.

OUR POLICY No timers. No "this might not be available tomorrow." The deadline is Jan 15. Everything else is a lie.
02.

Commission-optimized recommendations

Your agent gets paid 2.4% on Plan A and 4.8% on Plan B. Guess which one they recommend. Industry average: 82% of agents surfaced the higher-commission plan first in KFF's 2023 mystery-shopper study.

OUR POLICY Salaried agents. Same paycheck no matter what plan you pick — or if you pick nothing at all. Every quote is ranked by fit, not commission.
03.

Data resale to third parties

Your health profile is worth $40–$200 per record on the secondary data market. Many brokers sell it. Some sell it before you've even finished the quote form. You then receive calls for the next 18 months.

OUR POLICY Zero data resale. Ever. Contractually binding. We've turned down $6M in data-partnership revenue since 2023. Our CFO hates us. You shouldn't.
04.

Auto-renewal without comparison

Do nothing → you are moved to whatever the carrier's algorithm picks next. Usually a plan with a worse formulary and a higher deductible. Millions of Americans are silently re-enrolled this way every December.

OUR POLICY Never auto-renewed. Every November we email a side-by-side of your current plan vs. every option in your state. Opt-in only. Takes six minutes to review.
05.

47-page SBCs written by lawyers

The "Summary of Benefits and Coverage" is required by the ACA. It averages 47 pages, 11th-grade reading level, four fonts, two columns, no summary. It is a compliance document, not a user document.

OUR POLICY Every plan on Shophealthcare shows a one-page plain-English summary. The full SBC is linked, always, and we highlight the four things that actually matter for your situation.
